Business objective

Turn evaluation into a decision

Every Cato PoV proves something different — failover for one customer, TLS inspection coverage for another, shadow-AI discovery for a third. What should never differ is the shape of the engagement. A well-run PoV has four fixed properties: a scope both sides agreed before anything was built, success criteria written down as measurable statements, a timeline with an end date, and evidence collected as it lands rather than reconstructed at the end. Remove any one of them and the evaluation drifts into an open-ended trial that consumes SE time and customer goodwill without ever producing a verdict.

The failure mode

How a PoV becomes a free trial

Four ways an evaluation loses its way — each one avoidable, and each one common:

No success criteria

Impressive demos, no decision. If nobody wrote down what "proven" means, the evaluation cannot converge — every session ends with "interesting, can we also look at…" and the goalposts move weekly.

Unscoped drift

"Can it also…?" is a compliment — and a trap. Every untracked ask stretches the timeline, and by the time the answer is yes, the sponsor has forgotten why the evaluation started.

No cadence

Weeks pass between contacts. Findings are reconstructed from memory at the end instead of recorded as they land, the champion goes quiet, and momentum dies exactly when the evidence was strongest.

No decision meeting

The trial licence expires and nobody was ever asked to decide. The strongest evidence in the world does nothing if there is no meeting on the calendar where it is put next to the success criteria.

Ask yourself

"Before we switch anything on — what would you need to see proven, on your own traffic, for this evaluation to end in a decision? And who, besides you, has to see it?"

The framework

Five stages, one decision

The pipeline below is the whole method. Success criteria are agreed once, at the workshop, and travel with the PoV from that point on: the build exists to enable them, the test cycles exist to evidence them, and the wrap-up puts findings next to them. Anything raised mid-flight that is not on the register gets parked — visibly, respectfully, and in writing — in the next-steps register.

Success criteria agreed at the workshop carry through every stage — every weekly cycle lands evidence against a criterion, and the decision meeting reads the register, not memories.

  1. Scope & success criteria workshop

    One structured session before anything is built. Ask what the customer must see proven — then push every answer until it is testable, and map each one to a use-case page in this library (the library is the menu; see choosing the PoV use cases below). "We want to see our traffic" becomes "App Analytics shows top applications by user for the pilot site". Output: a list of success criteria, each with a measurement and a named owner on the customer side.

  2. Environment & prerequisites

    Agree the footprint: which sites and users are in scope (typically one branch socket plus a pilot group of remote users), which licences or trials are needed, and the two enablers that most often gate the timeline — TLS inspection where content-level proof is required (see TLS Inspection: Staged Rollout Playbook) and identity integration (see Identity Integration Design), because identity-attributed evidence is the difference between "an IP did this" and "Priya did this".

    Prerequisites gate the timeline — agree before week 1
    • Trial licences confirmed for every capability under test.
    • TLSi scope and exclusions approved by whoever owns privacy — before traffic is inspected, not after.
    • IdP integration connected and the pilot group syncing.
    • Change windows booked for connecting the pilot site.

  3. The test-case register

    Every success criterion becomes a row: what we test, how important it is, how it is measured, and where it stands. This is the single artefact the whole PoV runs on — reviewed at every sync, read out at the wrap-up. A worked sample:

    Use caseWhat we testPriorityMeasurement / evidenceStatus
    Branch SD-WAN resilience Pull the primary link on the pilot socket mid-call Must prove Voice call continues; Socket metrics show seamless failover Passed — wk 2
    Global app performance UK ↔ Dubai transfer over the backbone vs today's path Must prove Side-by-side transfer times; PoP-to-PoP latency from Topology In progress
    Threat prevention IPS & anti-malware on pilot-site traffic, monitor first Must prove Test threat blocked; event attributed to user and device in Events Scheduled — wk 3
    Remote-user ZTNA Pilot group works a full week through the Cato Client Should prove Experience Monitoring stats; no helpdesk tickets from the group In progress
    Shadow AI discovery AI app inventory on real traffic, monitor mode only Nice to have Discovery inventory and GenAI report export Scheduled — wk 3
    CASB tenant control Raised in week 2 — not in the agreed scope — — Parked — next-steps register

  4. Weekly cadence

    A 30-minute sync, same slot every week. Walk the register top to bottom, update statuses live on screen, and record findings as they land — a screenshot, an event export or a metric into the shared evidence doc, dated, against its criterion. New asks are triaged in the meeting: onto the register (with a criterion and a trade-off) or into the next-steps register. Evidence recorded weekly is what makes the wrap-up a formality rather than an archaeology project.

  5. Wrap-up & the decision meeting

    One page: findings against success criteria, pass by pass. Present it in a meeting booked at the workshop — with the economic buyer in the room, not just the technical champion. If a criterion failed, say so plainly; credibility is the product. The next-steps register — every parked ask — becomes the expansion roadmap, and for most engagements the natural next page is Migration Methodology & Co-existence Patterns.

Decision by design

The timeline ends in a meeting where the question is actually asked — a verdict is scheduled, not hoped for.

🧾

Evidence, not opinion

Every claim in the wrap-up has a dated screenshot, event or metric behind it, gathered while it happened.

📏

Scope discipline

New asks are honoured by parking them in writing — the customer feels heard and the PoV keeps its end date.
