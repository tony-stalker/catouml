Cloud Datacentre Integration

Most organisations run production workloads in AWS and Azure — yet those VPCs and VNets sit behind premium interconnect circuits and stacks of cloud virtual firewalls, islands with their own policy and their own blind spots. This use case makes the cloud datacentre just another site on the Cato WAN: three ways to connect (vSocket, IPsec, cross-connect), all live in the demonstration environment, all governed by the same policy and visibility as every physical site.

Business objective

Why this matters

The cloud is where the datacentre went. But while branches and physical DCs sit on the corporate WAN, the average VPC or VNet connects through a different stack entirely: a premium interconnect product back to a hub, virtual firewall appliances for security, and cloud-native tooling for whatever visibility exists. Every cloud region added repeats the pattern — more circuits, more appliances, more policy to keep in sync.

The objective: treat every VPC and VNet as just another site on the WAN — same policy, same visibility, same optimisation as Glasgow or the London DC. Branch users reach cloud workloads across the private backbone; the WAN firewall governs the path; traffic egresses at the Cato PoP nearest the cloud region, not across the public internet.

The problem today

Cloud DCs as islands

Two spending patterns show up in almost every cloud estate — and neither actually delivers WAN-grade connectivity or consistent security:

Premium interconnects

  • ExpressRoute / Direct Connect circuits per region, per hub
  • Recurring circuit and port charges on top of cloud spend
  • Provisioning lead times, contracts, carrier coordination
  • Connectivity only — no inspection, no policy on the path
  • Branch traffic still hairpins through a physical DC to get there

Cloud virtual firewalls

  • NGFW instances deployed and licensed per VPC/VNet
  • A second (or third) policy stack to author and audit
  • You own sizing, HA design, upgrades and failover testing
  • Separate logging — cloud flows invisible to WAN tooling
  • Policy drift between cloud and on-prem is the norm
Ask yourself

“What do your interconnect circuits and cloud firewall licences cost per year — and after all that spend, which single console shows the policy between a branch user and your production VPC?”

Connection options

Three ways in — all live in a demonstration environment

Cato gives you three ways to bring a cloud datacentre onto the WAN. They are not alternatives to choose once — most estates mix them by region and requirement, and your demonstration environment runs all three side by side.

MethodHow it connectsChoose it whenLive in a demonstration environment
vSocket Cato Socket as a virtual appliance inside the VNet/VPC, encrypted tunnels to the nearest PoP; deployable as an HA pair You want the full site experience — WAN optimisation, QoS, deep analytics — inside the cloud network “Azure-UKS-Hub-vSocket” (Azure, HA pair) · “AWS eu-west-2” (HA pair) · a second AWS vSocket (Dublin)
IPsec IKEv2 tunnels from the cloud provider’s native gateway straight to a Cato PoP — no VM footprint at all You already run Azure Virtual WAN / cloud VPN gateways, or want a region connected in minutes an Azure vWAN hub site · “aws-sydney-ipsec” (Sydney)
Cross-Connect Private layer-2 link between the cloud provider edge and the Cato PoP in the same facility — no tunnel overhead You need the highest throughput and deterministic latency into a major region a Cloud Interconnect site
Why this beats the premium products

Whatever the method, the cloud site lands on the same backbone, in the same policy set, with the same analytics as every physical site — and egress to the VPC happens at the PoP nearest the cloud region. No ExpressRoute or Direct Connect subscription, no virtual firewall fleet to size and patch.

The Cato solution

Cloud DCs as first-class WAN sites

Once connected, a cloud datacentre behaves exactly like the London DC: it appears in the topology, participates in WAN routing, is governed by the WAN firewall, and every flow to and from it is visible in App Analytics. A user in Glasgow reaching an app in the AWS VPC traverses the private backbone end to end — one policy decision, one audit trail, no internet middle mile.
