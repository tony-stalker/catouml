Business objective

Why this matters

The objective is a single-pane-of-glass view of networking, security, users, sites, devices, applications and traffic flows — eliminating the blind spots created by disparate point solutions, regional firewalls, VPN concentrators and carrier-managed links. Not just operational visibility for the engineer at 2am, but executive visibility with full context: usage trends, risk posture, SLA compliance and cost impact, on the same data.

The analyst view

“After decades of focusing on network performance and features, future network innovation will target operational simplicity, automation, reliability and flexible business models.” — Gartner, Strategic Roadmap for Networking, 2019. Visibility is where that simplicity is felt first: innovation is less about what the network does, and more about how it is operated.

The visibility gap

Stitched-together visibility fails everyone

Four complaints we hear from IT leaders in almost every meeting — all symptoms of the same fragmentation:

Poor visibility

“I have no idea what's going on in my network — so much in the shadow.” Answers require piecing together data from different technologies and service providers, and by the time the picture is assembled, it is out of date.

High complexity

Many point solutions address many, often overlapping use cases — and multiple MSPs each manage their own slice without a full picture. Nobody owns the end-to-end view.

Performance finger-pointing

Users complain about VoIP quality and laggy applications. Where do you begin? EUC, infrastructure, security, network and ops teams each check their own tool — and point at each other.

Inconsistent policy

Applying a uniform policy across separate firewall, network, DLP and IPS products is almost impossible and prone to misconfiguration — and no single console can even show you the drift.

Ask yourself

“When a user calls to say an application is slow, how many consoles do you open before you can say whether it's their device, the Wi-Fi, the last mile, the backbone or the application — and how long does that take?”

The Cato solution

One architecture, so one view

The single pane of glass is not a dashboard bolted on top of other products — it is a by-product of the architecture. Every edge — sites on Socket SD-WAN, remote users on the Cato Client, cloud datacentres on vSockets or IPsec — connects through the nearest PoP, where the Single Pass Cloud Engine (SPACE) processes each flow once with full networking and security context. That same context lands in one place: the Cato Management Application.

Traffic — all edges, all ports & protocols Single-pass context feed — identity · device · app · risk
Because all edges send all traffic through the PoPs, the telemetry was never split across boxes — the grey link is the same single-pass context feeding one console, not a collector you deploy.
