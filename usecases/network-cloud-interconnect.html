Cloud Interconnect: Dedicated Circuits to Cloud & DC

vSockets and IPsec tunnels bring most clouds onto the WAN in minutes — but some flows deserve a wire, not a tunnel. Cloud Interconnect sites land public clouds and private datacentres on the Cato backbone over a dedicated layer-2 circuit into the PoP: delivered through an exchange fabric such as Equinix Fabric or Megaport, or a physical cross-connect from a colo cage. Lower latency and higher bandwidth than the public internet — and the result is still just a site, with the same policy, visibility and backbone routing as every other site.

Business objective

When a tunnel isn't enough

For most cloud estates, a vSocket or an IPsec tunnel is exactly right: fast to deploy, encrypted end to end, and good for the vast majority of application traffic. But every tunnel-based on-ramp shares two properties that matter for datacentre-class flows: the last mile rides the public internet, and the traffic pays a tunnel tax — per-tunnel bandwidth ceilings and encryption overhead on every packet.

For storage replication, backup and restore, bulk data movement between DC and cloud, or latency-sensitive traffic between environments, the business wants what a leased line used to give it: deterministic latency, sustained high throughput, and a path nobody else's traffic can congest. Cloud Interconnect is how Cato delivers that — a dedicated private connection between two peers, giving lower latency and higher bandwidth than the public internet.

This use case complements the vSocket and IPsec story in Cloud Datacentre Integration — it does not replace it. Most estates mix on-ramps: tunnels for agility, interconnect circuits where the flows justify a wire.

How it works

One layer-2 front door into the backbone

Cloud Interconnect connects one network directly to another through a datacentre cloud exchange, delivered with a service-provider partner — Equinix (Cloud Exchange) Fabric or Megaport SDCI. A single VLAN ID is coordinated across all parties: the cloud provider, the colocation/exchange operator, and the Cato PoP. Two patterns use the same front door:

  • Public cloud — the provider's private-link service (the ExpressRoute / Direct Connect / Partner Interconnect / FastConnect class of service) lands on the exchange fabric, which cross-connects into the Cato PoP. Per-cloud Learning Center guides cover Azure, GCP and Oracle, and AWS is equally supported — the Cloud Interconnect site in your demonstration environment is an AWS one.
  • Private datacentre — a customer DC or colo cage connects via physical cross-connect or the exchange fabric into the nearest Cato PoP. No socket appliance, no IPsec tunnel anywhere in the path.

Either way, what lands in the CMA is simply a site of type “Cloud Interconnect” — a first-class citizen of the WAN with the same policy, the same visibility and the same backbone routing as every socket, vSocket and IPsec site.

Deployment path

From availability check to routed site

  1. Check PoP availability

    Cato publishes the list of Cloud Interconnect PoP locations and providers with provisioning lead times. Locations marked immediately available can be provisioned on demand from the CMA; the rest are coordinated with your Cato representative.

  2. Order the circuit

    For public cloud, order the provider's private-link service (the ExpressRoute / Direct Connect / Partner Interconnect / FastConnect class) landing on the exchange fabric — Equinix Fabric or Megaport. For a private DC, order the fabric port or a physical cross-connect from your cage.

  3. Coordinate the VLAN

    A VLAN ID is agreed across all parties — the cloud provider, the colocation/exchange operator and the Cato PoP — so the layer-2 handoff lines up end to end.

  4. Create the site in the CMA

    Network → Sites

    Create a new site and choose site type Cloud Interconnect — it sits alongside socket, vSocket and IPsec in the same site-creation flow.

  5. Route and secure like any site

    Declare the site's ranges, and the WAN firewall, network rules and analytics apply exactly as they do to every other site. No parallel policy domain for the circuit.

Set expectations on lead time

Provisioning lead times vary by location and provider — check the availability list early and put the circuit order at the front of the project plan, not the end.
